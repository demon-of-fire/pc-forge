# What is VRAM?

**VRAM**, or Video Random Access Memory, is a specialized type of memory used by your Graphics Processing Unit (GPU). Unlike system RAM, which is used by the CPU for general tasks, VRAM is dedicated solely to storing image data, textures, and frame buffers.

## Why does it matter?

As games and applications become more complex, they require more VRAM to store high-resolution textures and complex 3D models.

- **Gaming:** Higher resolution (like 4K) and higher texture quality settings directly increase VRAM usage. If you run out of VRAM, you may experience "stuttering" or significant drops in frame rates.
- **AI and Machine Learning:** Large Language Models (LLMs) and image generators (like Stable Diffusion) load their weights into VRAM. The more VRAM you have, the larger the models you can run locally.
- **Content Creation:** Video editing and 3D rendering use VRAM to store scene data and preview frames in real-time.

## How much do you need?

| Use Case | Recommended VRAM |
|---|---|
| 1080p Gaming | 8GB - 12GB |
| 1440p Gaming | 12GB - 16GB |
| 4K Gaming | 16GB+ |
| AI / LLMs | 12GB - 24GB+ |
| Professional 3D Work | 24GB+ |

**Bottom line:** VRAM is like a workspace. The bigger the desk, the more things you can have open at once without having to move them back and forth from the slower system RAM.
